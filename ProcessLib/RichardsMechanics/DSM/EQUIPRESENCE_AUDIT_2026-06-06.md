## 1. Executive summary

The DSM exchange is built so that one specific liquid chemical potential, `mu_lR`, couples the micro mass balance (which sets the interlayer water content `n_l`), the swelling eigenstress, and the macro pressure balance. Equipresence demands that this be **one** function of state wherever it appears. The audit confirms it is not: the Maxwell-conjugate stress term `mu_lR_mech` is added to `mu_lR` only at the two **global** exchange-assembly sites (`RichardsMechanicsFEM-impl.h:2727`, `:3590`), but is absent from the **three micro local-solve** sites that actually determine `n_l` (`:595`, `:762`, `:1088`/helper `:1002`-`1015`). The Gauss-point therefore converges `n_l` against a gate-closed `mu_lR` while the macro residual it feeds is evaluated with the gate-open `mu_lR` — two values of the same potential per GP, with disagreeing local/global Jacobians. This is the central equipresence violation, and it is self-flagged as pending in `MAXWELL_CONJUGATE_IMPLEMENTATION.md §7.2/§8` ("B1 PARTIAL"). Compounding it, the **sign** of `mu_lR_mech` drives micro water *in* under compression (verified two ways), the opposite of the documented "load EXPELS" goal — a contradiction forced by integrability against the (correct, settled) swelling-eigenstress sign, meaning the documented goal, not the code, is what must be revisited. Several tangent-consistency defects ride alongside: the implicit-`n_l` tangent linearizes the wrong (n_l-normalized vs REV-mass) residual; the Maxwell term's `p_L` sensitivity is dropped from the global p–p block when the gate opens; and the analytic vdW/EOS `n_l`-Jacobians freeze `n_S` even though the **production Task-13 PRJs select `current_porosity_split`** (`process-common.xml:69`), where `n_S = 1 - n_l` is live — the one defect that degrades the real Newton step on every production run today. Mitigating context: the Maxwell term is inert below a sharp gate that has never fired in any tested load path, so all gate-coupled defects are currently *latent*; but they become load-bearing the moment a run crosses the gate (precisely the high-stress regime tied to the Task-13 Π blow-up), and they must be closed before any gate-open result is trusted.

## 3. Central hypothesis — should `mu_lR` (and `rho_lR`) be functions of `p_film = p_disj + sigma_eff`, and how to wire it equipresently

### 3.1 What the audit actually establishes about the hypothesis

The hypothesis is that the interlayer (film) liquid potential is not a function of disjoining pressure alone but of a *film pressure* `p_film = p_disj + sigma_eff` — i.e. the mechanical (confining/effective) stress enters the same potential that drives the micro mass balance and the density EOS. The code already contains the machinery for exactly this: `computeMaxwellConjugateMicroPotential` adds a stress-derived increment `mu_lR_mech = S1·eps_v/rho_rev` to `mu_lR` (`PotentialExchange.h:282`), with `S1 = −n_S·(Π + n_l·dΠ/dn_l)` supplied from the eigenstress site. In integrability terms this is the *right* Maxwell half (`∂Ψ/∂n_l`) of the same free energy whose *left* half (`∂Ψ/∂eps_v = σ_sw`) is the swelling eigenstress; `MAXWELL_CONJUGATE_REV_REFERENCING.md` confirms `d(mu_lR_mech)/d(eps_v) = S1/(rho_lR·n_S)` matches `d(σ_sw,m)/d(m_l)`, so `(σ, mu_lR)` integrate from one `Ψ`. **The hypothesis is therefore already the design intent.** The audit's verdict is that it is *implemented half-way and with the gate-direction inverted relative to the stated goal*, not that it is wrong in principle.

Three constraints the audit pins down, which any correct wiring must respect:

1. **Single Ψ ⇒ single `mu_lR`.** If `mu_lR` is a function of `p_film`, then *every* appearance of `mu_lR` must use the `p_film` form — the micro `n_l` residual (`:604`-`605`, `:780`), its analytic Jacobian (`:656`-`657`), the `p_L_m`/eigenstress driver that reads stored `n_l`, and the macro exchange residual/Jacobian. Today only the last sees `p_film`; the first three see `p_disj` alone. This is the equipresence violation in §2 restated in hypothesis terms: a `mu_lR(p_film)` that is applied at the macro balance but not at the balance that *determines its own argument* `n_l` is not a single function — it is two functions sharing a name.

2. **The `(1 − phi_M)` area/referencing factor is not optional and is already (correctly) present.** `mu_lR` is a *specific* potential [J/kg]; its conjugate is the micro liquid *mass* per REV. The eigenstress references the aggregate (`σ_sw = −(1−phi_M)·n_l·Π`), so the conjugate half must divide by the REV liquid density `rho_rev = (1−phi_M)·rho_lR`, not `rho_lR` — scaling the conjugate up by `1/(1−phi_M)` (`PotentialExchange.h:247`-`254`, `:277`-`282`). This is the exact factor `MAXWELL_CONJUGATE_REV_REFERENCING.md` was written to fix, and the live `rho_rev` divisor implements it. **The hazard is downstream:** the *slope* `S1` (and the baseline `dmu_lR_dnl`) freeze `n_S = 1 − phi_M` while differentiating, which is exact only in `Reference` mode. In `current_porosity_split` (the production mode) `n_S = 1 − n_l`, so `d(1−phi_M)/dn_l ≠ 0` and the `(1−phi_M)` factor itself carries an `n_l`-derivative that the analytic tangents drop (§2 rows 4 and 5 of the medium table). A `p_film` wiring that re-uses these frozen-`n_S` slopes inherits this error.

3. **The sharp gate must become a smooth replacement.** The current term is a *snap-drain*: it switches on discontinuously at `p_conf ≥ phi_m·Π` (`:2723`/`:3586`) and is identically zero below. A discontinuous `mu_lR(p_film)` is hostile to Newton (the Jacobian has a delta at the gate) and is the proximate suspect for the Task-13 Π blow-up / SparseLU singular wall once a path crosses it. A `p_film = p_disj + sigma_eff` formulation makes the gate unnecessary: instead of a Boolean switch on `p_conf` vs `Π`, the effective-stress contribution enters `mu_lR` continuously, so the "expulsion" emerges smoothly as `sigma_eff` grows rather than firing all at once. The natural smooth surrogate is a regularised activation (e.g. `0.5·(1+tanh((p_conf−Π)/w))` or a `softplus`-weighted `(p_conf−Π)_+`) with width `w` set from the problem stress scale, replacing the `gate_open` Boolean while keeping the same asymptotes.

### 3.2 The sign caveat the hypothesis must resolve first

There is a genuine sign contradiction the hypothesis cannot wire around (§2, sign-convention row): with the settled, correct swelling sign `σ_sw,m = −n_S·n_l·Π < 0`, integrability forces `S1 > 0`, hence under compression (`eps_v < 0`) `mu_lR_mech < 0` and the micro phase *gains* water — the opposite of the documented "load expels" intent. **Verified two independent ways** (rate/residual and steady-state `rho_l_hat=0`). This is not a coding slip to patch; it is a statement that *adding `sigma_eff` with the integrability-consistent sign produces inflow under load*. Before wiring `mu_lR(p_film)` equipresently, the scientific call to make (yours, not the implementation's) is which of these is the physics: (a) the "load expels micro water / delamination" narrative (`MAXWELL_PAIR_RESTORATION.md §3`) — which would require `S1 < 0`, i.e. an *anti-swelling* contribution that breaks the verified swelling closure; or (b) the integrability-consistent inflow, in which case the documentation's expulsion language and the gate *direction* are what need revising. The audit's recommendation is to re-derive the sign against the `+α·(μ_LR − μ_lR)` residual convention the code actually ships and to lock it with the Maxwell-symmetry GP test (`§7.1`) and a stress-loading test (`§7.3`), both currently absent.

### 3.3 Equipresent wiring recipe for `mu_lR(p_film)` (and `rho_lR(p_film)`)

1. **One evaluator.** Extend `computeActiveMicroPotential` (`:1002`-`1015`) — or the `evaluate()`/`eval_at()` lambdas at `:589`/`:758`/`:1085` — to accept `sigma_eff` (equivalently `S1` + `eps_v` + `p_conf` + `n_S`) so that the *same* `mu_lR(p_film)` is returned to the micro residual, the eigenstress driver, and the macro exchange. Carry `sigma_eff`/`S1` in `PotentialExchangeLocalSolveContext` (`PotentialExchange.h:277`-`284`), which today lacks them.
2. **`rho_lR(p_film)` for free, consistently.** The reduced EOS `rho_lR` already depends on `n_l` (via `omega_l`, `:519`-`528`); once `n_l` is solved against `p_film`, `rho_lR` inherits the dependence automatically — *provided* the same `mu_lR(p_film)` drives the `n_l` solve. No separate `rho_lR(p_film)` path is needed; the equipresence fix to `mu_lR` propagates to `rho_lR` through `n_l`. (If a direct `p_film` term in the EOS is later wanted, mirror the `mu_lR` treatment: one evaluator, same `(1−phi_M)` referencing, same smooth gate.)
3. **Correct `(1 − phi_M)` area factor everywhere.** Keep the `rho_rev = (1−phi_M)·rho_lR` divisor (already correct), **and** make the slopes honest about `d(1−phi_M)/dn_l` when `mode == current_porosity_split`: add the `dmu_lR_dnS·(−1)` chain to `dmu_lR_dnl`/`dPi_dnl_mc` and the analogous EOS `domega/dnS·(−1)` chain to `drho_lR_dnl`, so the analytic tangent matches the live residual in the production mode.
4. **Smooth gate.** Replace the Boolean `gate_open = (p_conf ≥ Π)` (`PotentialExchange.h:271`) with a `C¹` activation of width `w(stress-scale)`; differentiate it analytically so `dmu_lR_mech_dp_conf` enters the tangent. This removes the snap-drain Jacobian delta and the gate-direction ambiguity collapses into a continuous monotone response (whose sign is fixed by step 3.2).
5. **One tangent.** With a single evaluator, the local Jacobian (`:656`-`657`, `:1311`-`1312`), `computeImplicitNlDpL` (rebuilt on the REV residual per §2), and the global p–p / p–u blocks (`:3604`-`3611`, `:3649`, `:3718`, `:3791`) all linearize the same `mu_lR(p_film)` — restoring transpose-symmetry between the eigenstress (p–u) and exchange (u–p) couplings that is currently half-present.
6. **Guard before trust.** Add the calibration-free GP tests (Maxwell-symmetry identity, per-mode FD `dn_l/dpL`, stress-loading expulsion/inflow direction) and run them with the gate forced open *before* any production run is allowed to cross the gate.
